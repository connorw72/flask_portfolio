How To Care For an Abandoned Pet

  • Can Help If You Have Lost A Pet Yourself

Click Any Of The Buttons Below For Information!

Steps:

1. Capture the dog safely without stressing it out or getting yourself hurt. Or if you cannot capture it take a picture or video
2. Keep the dog safe, fed well, and hydrated. Make sure anything you give the dog will not harm it.
3. Check if the dog has any identification on its collar and scan it for a microchip.
4. If you find identification alert the owners.
5. If you do not find identification bring the dog to a shelter and ask them to hold it until it's owner picks it up.

1. Check to see if the cat is feral. Otherwise, try and capture it with as little stress as possible.
2. Figure out if they are a stray or lost cat.
3. Check for a collar with a phone number and call the owners if it is present. Also, check for a microchip
4. Check lost and found ads posted.
5. If nothing works bring it to a shelter and ask them to hold it until the owner is found.

1. If the bird is in a tree try to get it to trust you with food that doesn't harm it.(Try to capture it)
2. Feed to bird food that is healthy for it and be sure it is hydrated.
3. Check the bird for identification in the form of a band on its leg or a microchip
4. If it has identification contact the owner and return the bird
5. If there is no identification look for lost pet notices around or if that doesn't work bring the bird to a bird rescue and rehabilitation organization

1. Ensure that the rabbit is domesticated and if so capture it with as little stress as possible
2. Keep the rabbit safe, well-fed, and hydrated. (Make sure the food you feed it is safe for it)
3. Contact pounds or rescues nearby to check if someone has notified of a lost rabbit
4. Check if it is microchipped and contact owners if it is.
5. If you are unable to catch the rabbit call Rabbit Run-Away Orphanage.
